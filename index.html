The Key Principles of the SDGs:

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are guided by the following key principles:

  • Universality: The SDGs apply to all countries, rich and poor, and aim to address global challenges.
  • Integration: The goals are interconnected, meaning achieving one goal can contribute to others.
  • Leave No One Behind: The SDGs emphasize ensuring that all people, especially the most vulnerable, are included in the progress made.

By focusing on these 17 goals, the SDGs aim to build a more just, inclusive, and sustainable world, addressing social, economic, and environmental dimensions of development.
