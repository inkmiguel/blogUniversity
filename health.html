Good Health and Well-Being

Good Health and Well-Being is the third Sustainable Development Goal (SDG 3) established by the United Nations. It aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.

📌 Key Facts

  • More than 6 million children under age 5 die each year, mostly from preventable causes.
  • Universal health coverage (UHC) is a key target of SDG 3.
  • Globally, maternal mortality has decreased by 38% between 2000 and 2017.
  • Access to vaccines and medicines remains a major challenge in developing countries.

🎯 Targets of SDG 3

  1. Reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to less than 70 per 100,000 live births.
  2. End the epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and other communicable diseases.
  3. Reduce premature mortality from non-communicable diseases through prevention and treatment.
  4. Ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive healthcare services.
  5. Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse.

🌐 Global Challenges

  • Poor infrastructure in rural areas.
  • Lack of healthcare professionals in developing countries.
  • High cost of medicines and services.
  • Emerging diseases and pandemics (e.g. COVID-19).

💡 How Can We Contribute?

  • Support organizations working in healthcare access and education.
  • Advocate for mental health awareness.
  • Promote healthy lifestyles (exercise, nutrition, sleep).
  • Encourage vaccinations and hygiene practices.

📊 Quick Stats (2023)

  • 800 million people spend at least 10% of their household budgets on health expenses.
  • Over 90% of the world’s population still breathes polluted air.
  • 1 in 3 people globally lack access to essential medicines.
